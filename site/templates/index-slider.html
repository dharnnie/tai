About TAI

The Afara Initiative prepares students for the dynamic nature of the 21st century job market.

MISSION

To address workforce incompetence, unethical leadership and unemployment by developing Intraprenuers and Entrepreneurs. We groom, empower and inspire young adults to achieve self-actualization, strive for excellence, become problem solvers who will ensure their communities are positively affected and ultimately changed by their contributions and decisions.

Learn more

VISION

To see a socially, economically and politically prosperous Nigeria, driven by ethical leaders, problem solvers, innovators and change agents that have the ability to revolutionize mindsets and positively change the status quo.

Quick Impact Numbers

Fellow flash & Testimonial

My friend and I started a small-scale recycling business. We built a hub on our school campus where we gather our materials from the eateries. We supply to a larger company. We recently did a documentary on our business venture with Channels Television Nigeria. Our dream is to get to the top of the chain, while learning through the process. The next plan is to use the recyclables and biodegradables to produce manure. We are still working very hard.

Odun Osineye

Environmental Entreprenuer

I am very excited to tell you that my teammates and I will be travelling to New York for the National Model United Nations (NMUN). Special thanks to TAI fellows Pelumi, Oyinda, Isabella, Ebi and Odun, and my mentor who assisted me in the fundraising, as we were able to raise N50K through a 2 weeks sales of the NMUN customized stickers. In the past weeks, we’ve been going through series of research, public presentation, resolution writing and caucusing training.

Gracious Ernest

Aspiring Lawyer/International Diplomat

The Afara Initiative set me up to imagine and think entrepreneurially and gave me real life experience to bring that imagination to life. Practically, during the post camp project at The Afara Initiative, my team – The Bridge Flour Mill Team identified a problem in the Food Industry, and that was hike in Baking flour prices, which has direct effect on the prices of its products. We came together to proffer a solution, and we developed the Potato flour, potentially making flour foods cheaper. As a result, we were awarded the ‘Most Viable Idea’ at the Presentation Awards. We have also received much positive feedback and even interest in investment. We are working hard to further develop our product and grow the business.

Kanu Victor

Entreprenuer/Food technologist

Due to the courage and confidence TAI gave me, I was also chosen as the Master of Ceremonies during the graduation programme at this social enterprise. I presently design and produce wears for more clients and I aim to set up my fashion house in the future.
